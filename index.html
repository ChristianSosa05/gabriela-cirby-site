Understanding 55+ Living

What is a 55+ community?

A 55+ community — also known as an active adult community — is a residential development legally designated under the Housing for Older Persons Act (HOPA) for residents aged 55 and older. At least 80% of occupied homes must have one resident who meets that age requirement.

These communities are designed around a specific way of living: less maintenance, more connection, more time for the things that actually matter to you — whether that's golf, travel, gardening, creative hobbies, or simply a peaceful place to call home.

From intimate townhome villages to resort-style master-planned developments with every imaginable amenity, 55+ communities vary enormously. Finding the right fit takes local knowledge and honest guidance. That's exactly what Gabriela provides.

Six Reasons People Choose 55+

It's not about slowing down.

The most common thing Gabriela hears from clients after moving in: "I wish I'd done this sooner." Here's why.

1

More time. Immediately.

When the HOA covers landscaping, exterior maintenance, and common areas, the weekend opens up in ways that surprise people. Suddenly there's time for the things you said you'd do "someday."

2

Neighbors who get it.

Living among people at a similar stage of life means shared interests, natural conversation, and genuine friendships that form faster than in mixed-age neighborhoods. Loneliness diminishes significantly in active adult communities.

3

Amenities at your front door.

Pools, fitness centers, clubs, classes, pickleball courts, community gardens, art studios. For many, the activity calendar alone changes daily life in profound ways.

4

Security and peace of mind.

Many 55+ communities are gated, with active security and a shared investment in the community's wellbeing that goes beyond what most standard neighborhoods offer.

5

Homes designed for how you live now.

Single-story layouts, wider doorways, no-step entries, accessible bathrooms, and thoughtful floor plans are built-in — not retrofitted. The home works for you.

6

A sound financial decision.

55+ communities tend to hold their value well. The predictability of what your home demands — financially and physically — reduces stress significantly in retirement.
